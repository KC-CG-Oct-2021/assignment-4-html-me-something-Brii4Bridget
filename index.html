UNDERSTANDING THE AFRICA REGION:
Africa IS NOT a Country!

The Five Regions of Africa

The Continent of Africa, much like other continents around the world, is divided into Regions named primarily by there location on the Continent. Each with varying cultures, resources and beauty! Much like the Countries, on the North America Continent, Canada and the United States of America, each country on the Africa continent has its on defined Provinces (States), and, further defined Jurisdictions such as Cities, Municipalities (Counties), and more Local Zoning.

How Many Countries on the CONTINENT of AFRICA?

Well, the simple and the most accepted answer to this question is that in total there are 54 independent sovereign countries in Africa with their Capital states and are recognized by both the African Union as well as the United States. Further, Africa is the second largest continent in the world and is a homeland to 54 i.e. the largest number of countries within a continent in the world. The Atlantic Ocean binds it on the west, the red sea and Indian ocean on the east, the Mediterranean Sea on the west and the mingling waters of Indian and Atlantic Ocean on the south. Africa accounts for a total land area of about 30,365,000 sq. km or 11,724,000 sq. mi. and extends to about 7,400 km from east to west and 8,000 km from north to south.
